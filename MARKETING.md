# Marketing Foundation

## The Core Insight

Every expense tracker fails the same way: it asks you to change your behavior. Open the app. Tap the category. Enter the amount. Tap save. Nobody keeps doing this.

[BRAND] flips the model. It lives in Telegram — where you already are — and understands plain English. You just text it like a friend. That friend is Stash.

---

## The Problem We're Solving

### How people track expenses today (and why they stop)

**Spreadsheets**
You build a beautiful Excel sheet. Columns, formulas, color coding. You use it for two weeks. Then life gets busy, you miss a few days, and the gap feels too big to fill in. The sheet sits there, a monument to good intentions.

**PDF statements**
You download your bank statement at the end of the month. Scroll through 60 lines. Try to remember what "POS DEBIT 4821" was. Realize you spent $340 you can't account for. Close the tab. Feel bad.

**Connecting your bank**
Apps like Mint, YNAB, and every fintech startup want your banking credentials or read-only bank access. You either don't trust it, your bank blocks it, or you have 3 accounts and a credit card and the sync breaks every other week. And they still miss cash.

**Other budgeting apps**
You download the app. You get through onboarding. You're assigned 12 categories you didn't choose. You can't add "Boba Tea" as a category. You're forced to put it under "Dining Out" which feels wrong. You stop caring. You delete the app.

**The real problem:** None of these tools meet you where you are. They all require effort upfront and sustained discipline. Most people aren't undisciplined — the tools are just badly designed.

## Pain Point Breakdown (for copy and ads)

### Pain 1: "I have to connect my bank and I don't trust it"
Other apps want your login credentials or bank read access. [BRAND] doesn't touch your bank. You tell Stash what you spent, when you spent it. He knows what you tell him — nothing more. No OAuth, no Plaid, no "we take security seriously" disclaimers.

**Copy angle:** *"No bank connection required. Ever."*

### Pain 2: "I have multiple accounts and nothing syncs right"
Credit card, debit, cash, a second card for travel points — syncing tools break constantly, miss transactions, or double-count. [BRAND] works across all of them because Stash doesn't care where the money came from. You just say what you spent.

**Copy angle:** *"Cash, card, or credit — if you spent it, text it."*

### Pain 3: "I download PDF statements and try to piece it together at month end"
This is retroactive guilt, not financial awareness. By the time you're reading a statement, it's too late to change anything. Stash gives you live budget status every time you log. You know where you stand today, not 30 days from now.

**Copy angle:** *"Know where you stand today. Not at month end."*

### Pain 4: "I built a spreadsheet and stopped using it after two weeks"
Spreadsheets require you to remember to open them, remember what you spent, and do the math yourself. [BRAND] is always open — it's Telegram. You log immediately when you spend, so nothing gets forgotten. Stash does all the math.

**Copy angle:** *"The spreadsheet you'll actually keep up with."*

### Pain 5: "Other apps don't let me create my own categories"
You have specific spending patterns. Maybe you track "Date Nights" separately from "Dining Out". Maybe you want a "Side Project" category. [BRAND] lets you add any category you want, and Stash matches your spending to it automatically.

**Copy angle:** *"Your categories, not ours."*

### Pain 6: "I forget to log things and then the data is useless"
If you've gone quiet, Stash checks in at the end of the day. Not a push notification you swipe away — a Telegram message you can reply to immediately. One message and you're back on track.

**Copy angle:** *"Stash notices when you've gone quiet. And he has thoughts about it."*

## Features

### Log by texting
Text what you spent in any format. Stash extracts the amount, description, category, and merchant automatically.

```
coffee 5.50
lunch with client $34
Uber to airport 28 dollars
split groceries 80 — half mine
```

### Live budget status on every log
Every time you log, Stash shows your month total, budget remaining, and real spendable amount (budget minus savings goal minus what you've spent). No math. No spreadsheet. Just clarity.

```
Stash: Logged $5.50 · Food
       March total: $312.40
       Budget remaining: $187.60
       Spendable today: $62.60
```

### Stash checks in at the end of the day
If you haven't logged anything by evening, Stash sends a nudge. Reply right there in Telegram and your day is captured. No broken streaks, no missing data.

```
Stash: Hey — nothing logged today. Spend anything?
       I won't judge. (Much.)

You:   dinner 45, gas 60

Stash: Logged both. March total now $418.40.
       Still under budget. Good.
```

### Ask anything in plain English
No menus. No date pickers. No filter dropdowns. Just ask Stash.

```
You: how much did I spend on food this week?
You: what were my top 3 spending categories last month?
You: show me everything from Tuesday
You: how close am I to my budget?
```

### Custom categories
Add any category you want. Stash uses fuzzy matching to automatically assign expenses to the right one, even with typos or variations.

### Savings goals
Set a monthly savings target. Stash shows your real spendable amount — budget minus savings goal minus spent. Not just "what's left" but "what's safe to spend."

### Undo and delete
Made a mistake? Say "undo" and it's gone. Stash confirms:
```
Stash: Gone. Never happened.
       (It did happen, but the data doesn't know that anymore.)
```

### No bank connection. Ever.
You tell Stash what you spent. He tracks it. Your bank credentials stay with your bank.

## Objection Handling (FAQ)

**"Do I need to download something?"**
No. [BRAND] is a Telegram bot. If you have Telegram, you're ready. Just tap the link and start texting Stash.

**"Do you connect to my bank?"**
Never. You tell Stash what you spend manually. This is intentional — no credentials, no third-party bank access, no sync failures. You're in full control.

**"What if I have multiple accounts?"**
It doesn't matter. Cash, debit, credit, PayPal — just text what you spent from whatever account. Stash tracks the spending, not the account.

**"Can I use my own categories?"**
Yes. Add any category you want. Stash will automatically match your expenses to it going forward.

**"Is my financial data safe?"**
Your expenses are stored in a private database tied only to your Telegram user ID — no name, no email, no bank connections. Stash never sees your actual accounts or balances.

**"What if I make a mistake?"**
Say "undo" — Stash deletes the last entry. Or say "recent" to see your last 10 and delete any specific one.

**"What if I forget to log things?"**
Stash checks in at the end of the day if you've gone quiet. Reply right there in Telegram to catch up.

**"Does it work in Canada / with CAD?"**
Yes. All amounts are in Canadian dollars by default.

**"What if I want to cancel?"**
Cancel anytime. No lock-in.
