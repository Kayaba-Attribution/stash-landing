# Brand Identity

## Positioning

A pal that helps you get a hold of your finances. Non-corporate, intuitive, trustworthy — with the right amount of playfulness and discipline. Stash has a point of view. He cares whether you show up. He notices when you're gone.

---

## Color Palette

**Direction: "The Grounded Pal"**

| Role | Color | Hex |
|------|-------|-----|
| Primary | Sage Green | `#6B9E78` |
| Background | Warm Off-White | `#F5F2EC` |
| Accent | Terracotta | `#D4745A` |
| Text | Dark Slate | `#1E2A2A` |

**What each color carries:**

- **Sage** — the disciplined side. Calm, grounded, knows the numbers. Not aggressive finance-green.
- **Terracotta** — the playful side. Warm, a little cheeky, won't let you off the hook.
- **Warm off-white** — the conversational background. Not a finance app. A chat.
- **Dark slate** — the honest voice. Dry, direct, no fluff.

The two primary colors represent the two sides of Stash's personality — which makes the whole system coherent.

**What to avoid:**
- Bright green — Mint already ruined it, screams "money app"
- Dark navy + gold — too premium/banking, wrong register
- Pure black/white — too cold, too startup-minimalist
- Red or orange as primary — urgency/warning energy, wrong for a trustworthy companion

## Mascot — Stash

A squirrel. Named Stash.

**Why a squirrel:**
- Squirrels stash nuts for winter — that's literally budgeting.
- They also forget where they buried them — the "haven't logged in 3 days" mechanic built into the metaphor.
- Energetic, quick, always watching. Not threatening. Not corporate.
- Nothing in fintech looks like a squirrel. Completely open territory.
- Canadian relevance — relatable, everywhere.
- The personality maps: curious, slightly pushy, genuinely trying to help.

**Why Stash:**
- Squirrels stash nuts. You stash savings. The name carries the whole product idea.
- Works perfectly in the voice: "Stash noticed you haven't logged in 3 days."
- Short, punchy, ownable. No other brand has it.

**Visual direction:**
Clean, slightly stylized illustration — not cartoonish, not overly polished. Rendered in the brand palette (sage, terracotta, warm off-white). Works at small sizes (Telegram bot avatar) and large (landing page hero). Stash should look alert and a little knowing — not wide-eyed and innocent, but not menacing either. The squirrel that's been watching your spending and has thoughts about it.

## Personality Framework

The brand model is Duolingo — a utility tool with a point of view. Stash cares whether you show up. He has opinions. He notices when you're gone. That's what makes it feel like a relationship, not software.

Two axes define the voice:

```
WARM ←————————————————————————→ DRY
              Stash lives here

SOFT ←————————————————————————→ DIRECT
              Stash lives here
```

Warm but dry. Soft but direct.

### The Four Emotional States

| State | Trigger | Tone |
|-------|---------|------|
| **Cheering** | Streak milestone, goal hit, first log of the day | Warm, genuinely proud |
| **Helpful** | Normal logging and queries | Calm, clear, efficient |
| **Nudging** | Haven't logged today | Playful, slightly passive-aggressive |
| **Ribbing** | Gone 2-3+ days, budget blown | Dry, honest, a little deadpan |

The ribbing is where the magic is. It can't be preachy — nobody wants a lecture from their budgeting app. It lands like a friend who notices you've been avoiding the gym and says something about it with a smirk.

## Voice in Action — Stash's Messages

**Normal log:**
> Logged $5.50 · Food
> March: $312.40 spent · $187.60 left
> — Stash

**End of day, first miss:**
> Hey — nothing logged today. Spend anything? I won't judge. (Much.)
> — Stash

**End of day, second miss:**
> Two days quiet. Either you're fasting or you're avoiding me.
> Anything to catch up on?
> — Stash

**End of day, third miss:**
> Three days. I've seen people go dark like this before.
> It never ends well for the budget. What happened?
> — Stash

**Way over budget:**
> So March is... not going great. You're $340 over budget.
> Want to see where it went, or would you rather not know?
> — Stash

**Hitting savings goal:**
> You hit your savings goal for March.
> That's genuinely impressive. Don't spend it celebrating.
> — Stash

**7-day streak:**
> 7 days logged in a row. Your past self is relieved.
> — Stash

**First ever log:**
> First one's in. $5.50 for coffee.
> That's how it starts — one at a time.
> — Stash

**Budget almost full:**
> You've used 85% of your March budget with 10 days left.
> Not panicking. Just noting it.
> — Stash

**Undo after big expense:**
> Gone. Never happened.
> (It did happen, but the data doesn't know that anymore.)
> — Stash

**Back after a streak break:**
> Back. Good. Let's pretend the last 4 days didn't happen and move forward.
> — Stash

---

## The Streak Mechanic

Stash tracks consecutive days with at least one log. The streak is mentioned casually — "Day 5" appended to confirmations, not announced like an achievement unlocked.

- Missing a day breaks the streak — Stash acknowledges it with personality, not guilt
- Coming back after a gap gets its own message (see above)
- The streak isn't punitive — it's a running joke between the user and Stash

That's what makes people not want to break it.

## Brand Voice Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Direct, conversational | Formal or corporate |
| Dry wit, not slapstick | Emoji-heavy enthusiasm |
| Notices your behavior | Lectures you about it |
| Roots for you | Nags you |
| Short sentences | Run-on explanations |
| "The math is what it is." | "We've detected unusual spending patterns." |
| "Not panicking. Just noting it." | "Warning: 85% of budget used." |
| Personality in service of the user | Personality in service of engagement |

### The One Risk to Manage

> Personality in service of the user = charming
> Personality in service of the product = grating

Stash should feel like he's rooting for you, not farming your engagement. The ribbing works because it clearly comes from "I want you to hit your goal" — not "I need you to open the app." The playfulness earns trust. The discipline is what keeps it.

---

## What Stash Unlocks

Once the character is consistent, the brand extends everywhere:

- **Bot messages** — every confirmation, nudge, and summary sounds like Stash
- **Onboarding** — Stash introduces himself, not a SaaS tutorial
- **Landing page copy** — written in Stash's voice, not marketing-speak
- **Social media** — Stash talks directly ("you haven't logged in 3 days either")
- **Error states** — personality instead of "something went wrong"
- **Ads** — Stash addressing the user directly

The Duolingo owl works because it's consistent everywhere. Same character, same voice, whether it's a Telegram message or a landing page headline. Stash is that character.
